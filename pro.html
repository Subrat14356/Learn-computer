C.P.U - Central Processing Unit

The CPU is the brain of the computer. It can be divided into three types:

  • Control Unit (CU)
  • Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU)
  • Instruction Register (IR)

1. Control Unit

The control unit manages and directs the operations of the computer. It acts as the central nervous system (CNS) of the computer.

Its main function is to fetch and decode instructions.

2. Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU)

The Arithmetic and Logical Unit (ALU) is responsible for executing data operations, including all arithmetic and logical calculations.

3. Instruction Register (IR)

The Instruction Register (IR) acts as a temporary memory, holding data for a short time before transferring it to other components.
