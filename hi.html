History of Computer

1. Abacus

The word "Abacus" originates from the Greek word "abax," meaning "board or table."

Abacus was one of the earliest calculating devices.

It is made of a frame, rods, and beads.

Using the abacus, calculations like addition and subtraction can be performed by moving beads along the rods.

2. John Napier's Bones

In 1617, Scottish mathematician John Napier invented Napier's Bones.

It consisted of nine rods with numbers marked on them, used for calculations.

It helped in multiplying and dividing large numbers more easily.

3. Pascaline

In 1642, Blaise Pascal developed a mechanical calculator known as the Pascaline.

It was one of the world's earliest adding machines, capable of performing calculations up to large numbers.

4. Difference Engine

In the 1800s, Charles Babbage designed the Difference Engine.

This device could perform mathematical calculations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

In 1833, he proposed the Analytical Engine, which led to him being called the "Father of the Computer."
